Why Consultation Matters More Than Ever in 2026

The return to 30-day visa-exempt entries in May 2026 sounds simple. It is not. Combined with the DTV requirements, TM30 obligations, and the new TDAC digital arrival process, the paperwork landscape for a new arrival is more layered than it has been in years. One wrong step and you are on an unplanned border run within your first month.

The VIP Concierge service is built around direct access to someone who has navigated this system for 16 years. Not a PDF guide. Not a Facebook group. A person you can message when the landlord changes the contract terms the night before you sign, or when your airport transfer does not show.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Guru consultation actually cover?

Pre-arrival hours focus on visa documents (DTV, LTR, or tourist visa planning), neighbourhood selection based on your budget and lifestyle, and any logistics you want to clarify before landing. On-arrival hours cover real-time situations: landlord meetings, contract review, scooter rental vetting, or anything that comes up on the ground. Time is tracked honestly. Unused hours do not carry over but are rarely wasted.

Is the DTV still the best visa for long-term stays in 2026?

For most remote workers and retirees, yes. The DTV (Destination Thailand Visa) allows 180 days per entry and is renewable. With the return to 30-day visa-exempt entries in May 2026, the DTV is more relevant than ever for anyone planning to stay longer than a month without doing constant border runs. The Ambassador and VIP tiers include a dedicated DTV strategy session to assess your specific situation.

Can I use consultation hours for TM30 and 90-day reporting help?

Yes. TM30 filing and 90-day reporting are common use cases, especially in the first month. We can walk through the online system together, confirm your address registration is correct, and flag any issues before they become fines. Full guide also available: 90-Day Reporting and TM30 in Chiang Mai.

Frequently Asked Questions

What concierge and personal assistant services are available in Chiang Mai?

A range of English-speaking personal assistant and concierge services operate in Chiang Mai catering to expats and high-value visitors. Services typically cover airport transfers, property finding, visa run organisation, translation for government processes, personal shopping, and event planning. Recommendations from the expat community on Facebook are the most reliable source of current providers.

How much does a personal assistant cost in Chiang Mai?

Freelance English-Thai bilingual personal assistants typically charge 500 to 1,500 THB per hour depending on task complexity. Full-day rates run 2,000 to 6,000 THB. For tasks requiring Thai language skills and local knowledge such as immigration, property negotiation, or medical translation, professional rates are justified by the value of getting the outcome right.

Is it worth using a visa agent in Chiang Mai?

For complex visa categories (LTR, Non-Immigrant B, DTV applications), a reputable visa agent who knows the current process at Chiang Mai Immigration can save significant time and prevent costly mistakes. For straightforward 90-day reporting and simple extensions, the DIY process is manageable. The cost of getting a complex visa wrong is generally higher than the agent fee.

Can a concierge service help with property rentals in Chiang Mai?

Yes. Local concierge services with Thai language capability can access properties not listed on English-language platforms, negotiate directly with landlords in Thai, review lease agreements, and advise on areas and pricing. For newcomers unfamiliar with Chiang Mai's rental market, a local guide who knows current pricing prevents overpaying for accommodation.

What should I look for when choosing a concierge or PA in Chiang Mai?

Thai language fluency is essential for anything involving government processes or local negotiation. References from other expats carry more weight than website testimonials. Clarity on how they charge and experience with your specific task type. The Chiang Mai Expats Club Facebook group has recommendations for trusted service providers across all categories.

Guru Tip

For immigration and visa processes specifically, use someone who has done your exact visa type at the Chiang Mai office recently, not just someone who is generally helpful. Thai immigration processes change frequently and what worked six months ago may require different documentation today. Ask specifically: have you done a DTV or LTR application at Chiang Mai Immigration in the last 3 months? Recency of experience matters more than general competence for Thai government processes.
