Red Cross Blood Bank Chiang Mai for helping others in Thailand.

The Red Cross Blood Bank Chiang Mai is where you can make blood donations to help save lives. Every donation can help up to three people.

Category Home / Giving Back
Read time 8 min
Last updated May 2026

Quick Facts

  • Location: Maharaj Nakhon Chiang Mai Hospital (Suan Dok), Suthep Road
  • Hours: Mon to Fri 8:30am to 3:30pm, Sat 8:30am to noon
  • Closed: Sundays and public holidays
  • Process time: 45 to 90 minutes first visit
  • Bring: Passport, eat 2 hours before, drink water
  • No appointment: Walk-in
  • Gifts: Small refreshment pack typically given after donation

The Thai Red Cross Blood Bank in Chiang Mai is located within the Maharaj Nakhon Chiang Mai Hospital complex (commonly called Suan Dok Hospital) on Suthep Road. It is one of the few places in the city where donating blood as a foreigner is straightforward: the staff are accustomed to foreign donors, English language assistance is available, and the process follows international blood bank standards.

Chiang Mai's blood bank serves not only local hospitals but also supplies blood to the region's medical network. Donations by residents, including foreign residents and long-stay visitors, contribute directly to a supply that serves the Northern Thailand population.

Location and How to Get There

The Thai Red Cross Blood Bank Chiang Mai branch is inside Suan Dok Hospital on Suthep Road, to the west of the Old City. The hospital campus is large and the blood bank building is signposted within the grounds. When entering from the main gate off Suthep Road, follow the signs for the blood bank or ask at the information desk near the main entrance.

By motorbike or car: Suthep Road runs along the south side of the CMU campus. Parking is available in the hospital grounds. By songthaew: Red trucks running along Suthep Road or heading to the university pass the hospital. By Grab or rideshare: give the destination as Maharaj Nakhon Chiang Mai Hospital (Suan Dok).

Opening Hours

The blood bank operates Monday to Friday from 8:30am to 3:30pm and Saturday from 8:30am to approximately 12 noon. The bank is closed on Sundays and Thai public holidays. Last admission for donation is typically 3pm on weekdays to allow time for completion before closing. Arriving before 2pm gives comfortable time.

The Donation Process: What to Expect

There is no appointment required. The process is walk-in. On arrival, you register at the reception desk. First-time donors will complete a longer health questionnaire and have their donor record established. Return donors provide their donor card or passport for record lookup.

Pre-Donation Screening

A nurse takes your blood pressure, pulse, and weight. A finger-prick blood sample is taken to measure haemoglobin levels. If any result falls outside acceptable ranges, you are deferred and given guidance on when to return. If all readings are within limits, you proceed to donation.

The Donation

Whole blood donation takes approximately 8 to 12 minutes. You lie on a reclinable chair in the donation room. The needle insertion is a single prick. Staff monitor throughout. After the blood is collected, the needle is removed and light pressure is applied. You remain seated briefly to ensure you feel stable before moving.

Post-Donation

After donating, you move to a rest area where refreshments are provided. The Thai Red Cross typically gives donors a small gift pack including snacks and a beverage. Sit for at least 10 to 15 minutes before standing. Drink the fluids provided. Most people feel completely normal within 20 to 30 minutes of donating.

What to Bring

  • Passport (required for registration and donor ID, especially for first-time foreign donors)
  • Donor card if you have donated in Chiang Mai before
  • Have eaten 2 to 3 hours before. A normal meal, nothing fatty.
  • Be well hydrated. Drink extra water the morning of your donation.

What to Do and Avoid After Donating

For the remainder of the day after donation: drink extra fluids, avoid heavy exercise or physical exertion, avoid alcohol, and keep the bandage on for at least 2 hours. If you feel light-headed after leaving the blood bank, sit down immediately and wait until the feeling passes before continuing. This is uncommon but can happen, especially if you did not eat well before donating.

Why Donate Blood in Chiang Mai

The Northern Thailand region has a consistent need for blood supply. The Chiang Mai Red Cross blood bank serves hospitals and emergency services across the region. Accidents, surgeries, and medical emergencies in Chiang Mai and the surrounding provinces depend on the supply maintained by the blood bank. Long-term foreign residents who are eligible have a straightforward opportunity to contribute to the community they live in.

Key Takeaways

Key Takeaways

  • Location: Thai Red Cross Blood Bank inside Suan Dok Hospital, Suthep Road, Chiang Mai.
  • Hours: Mon to Fri 8:30am to 3:30pm, Sat 8:30am to noon. Closed Sundays and holidays.
  • No appointment. Walk in with your passport and having eaten 2 to 3 hours before.
  • First-time visit takes 45 to 90 minutes including registration, screening, donation, and rest period.
  • Foreign donors are accepted. English language assistance is available.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is the Red Cross Blood Bank in Chiang Mai?

Inside Maharaj Nakhon Chiang Mai Hospital (Suan Dok Hospital) on Suthep Road, west of the Old City near the CMU campus. The blood bank building is signposted within the hospital grounds. Enter from the main gate on Suthep Road and follow the internal signs or ask at the main reception.

Can I donate blood at the Chiang Mai Red Cross without an appointment?

Yes. The blood bank is entirely walk-in. No advance booking is required. Arrive before 3pm on weekdays or before 11:30am on Saturdays to allow enough time for the full process before the bank closes. Early morning on weekdays is typically less busy.

How long does it take to donate blood in Chiang Mai?

The actual blood collection takes 8 to 12 minutes. The total time from arrival to departure including registration, health screening, donation, and the post-donation rest period is 45 to 90 minutes for a first-time donor. Return donors who are already registered are typically done in 45 to 60 minutes.

Do I need to speak Thai to donate blood at the Chiang Mai Red Cross?

No. English-language assistance is available at the Chiang Mai blood bank. The health questionnaire and screening process can be conducted in English. Many of the staff have experience working with foreign donors. Bring your passport and the communication is manageable without Thai language skills.

What do I get for donating blood in Thailand?

A small refreshment pack with snacks and a drink is typically provided by the Thai Red Cross after donation. Some donation drives offer additional small gifts. There is no cash payment for blood donation in Thailand. The primary return is the knowledge that your donation contributes to the regional blood supply for emergencies and surgeries across Northern Thailand.

Guru Tip

Go on a weekday morning rather than a Saturday. Saturday is popular because it is the only weekend option, which means the queue is longer and the wait time before donation is extended. A Tuesday or Wednesday morning arrival around 9am means you are usually done within an hour total. The post-donation refreshments are the same regardless of which day you go, and the quieter weekday atmosphere is more comfortable for first-time donors who are not sure what to expect.
