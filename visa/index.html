Common Visa Types Overview

Here's a quick reference for visa types commonly used by people relocating to Chiang Mai. For detailed information on requirements, timelines, and applications, visit CMLocals.

Tourist Visa (60 days)

The most common entry visa for short-term stays. Valid for 60 days from date of issue. Can be extended for additional 30 days in-country. Good for initial exploration or short visits.

Learn more at CMLocals

ED Visa (Education)

For those enrolling in Thai language schools or approved educational programs. Renewable annually. Popular with digital nomads and lifelong learners.

Learn more at CMLocals

Retirement Visa (1 year)

For those age 50+ with financial requirements (either 800,000 THB savings or 65,000 THB monthly income). Must show funds in Thai bank. Renewable annually.

Learn more at CMLocals

Digital Nomad Long Stay Visa (DTV) — 180 days

New visa type for remote workers and digital nomads. Valid for 180 days. Growing in popularity for location-independent workers.

Learn more at CMLocals

Multiple Tourist Visas (Visa Run Strategy)

Entering on Tourist visa, then exiting and re-entering Thailand ("visa runs") to reset the 60-day clock. Possible but requires frequent border trips. See CMLocals for current regulations.

Quick Visa Questions

How long can I stay in Thailand without a visa?

Citizens of most countries receive 30 days visa-free entry by air, or 15 days by land border. For longer stays, a visa is required.

Can I extend my visa in Thailand?

Yes. Tourist visas can be extended for 30 additional days at immigration offices in Thailand. Other visa types have specific extension processes. See CMLocals for details.

What happens if I overstay my visa?

Overstaying has penalties including fines (500 THB per day, max 20,000 THB) and potential ban from Thailand. Always track visa expiry dates.

Which visa is best for digital nomads?

The new Digital Nomad Long Stay Visa (DTV) is ideal for remote workers. ED Visa is another option. Tourist visa + extensions works for shorter stays. See CMLocals for comparison.

Do I need a Thai bank account for visa extension?

Many visa types require Thai bank account or proof of funds. Requirements vary by visa. See CMLocals for your specific visa type.
