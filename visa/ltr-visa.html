LTR Visa Thailand: Long Term Resident Visa

Elite members, wealthy investors, high income earners, and professionals. Multiple pathways to long-term residency. Requirements, costs, application process.

TL;DR: What You Need to Know

  • LTR: Long Term Resident visa. Multiple pathways for Elite members, wealthy individuals, income earners, professionals.
  • Most common: Elite membership (expensive upfront), Wealth-based (3M THB), Income-based (80K THB/month).
  • 4-year initial grant. Renewable for additional 4-year periods.
  • Easiest pathway depends on your financial situation.
  • Most prestigious long-stay visa in Thailand.
  • See CMLocals.com/ltr-visa for official requirements.

What Is LTR Visa

LTR stands for "Long Term Resident" visa. It's Thailand's premier long-stay visa designed for wealthy individuals, business leaders, skilled professionals, and high-net-worth individuals.

Unlike other long-stay visas, LTR offers longer initial validity (4 years instead of 1 year), prestigious status, and multiple pathways to qualify based on wealth, income, or professional credentials.

LTR Pathways

Elite Program

Thailand Elite membership grants LTR visa automatically. Membership costs 600,000 THB to 2 million+ THB depending on package. Includes visa, concierge services, and other benefits. Most expensive pathway but fastest approval.

Wealth-Based (3 Million THB)

Deposit 3 million THB (approximately USD 91,000 or AUD 150,000) in Thai bank account. Keep funds in Thailand. Receive 4-year LTR visa. Funds must stay in Thai banking system. Easiest self-funded option.

Income-Based (80,000 THB Monthly)

Prove 80,000 THB (approximately USD 2,400 or AUD 3,300) monthly income from outside Thailand. Provide employment letters, tax returns, or business documentation. Good for high-income earners who don't want to tie up capital.

Professional/Expert Category

For specialists in fields like technology, finance, healthcare, or skilled trades. Requirements vary by profession. Must demonstrate expertise and contribution to Thailand. Application more competitive but no large fund requirement.

Who Qualifies

Each pathway has different requirements, but general criteria:

  • Age 20 or older
  • Clean criminal record and immigration history
  • Stable financial situation (proof varies by pathway)
  • Valid passport (at least 6 months validity)

Application Process

Elite Membership Pathway

1) Apply to Thailand Elite program. 2) Pay membership fee. 3) Receive LTR visa with concierge support. 4) Fastest pathway (2-4 weeks processing).

Wealth-Based Pathway (3M THB)

1) Open Thai bank account. 2) Deposit 3 million THB. 3) Let funds settle (3 months typical). 4) Apply at Immigration with bank statements. 5) Processing: 5-7 days. 6) Receive 4-year permission.

Income-Based Pathway (80K/month)

1) Gather proof of 80K+ monthly income (employment letter, tax returns, bank statements). 2) Prepare documents. 3) Apply at Immigration. 4) Processing: 3-5 days. 5) Receive 4-year permission.

Professional/Expert Pathway

1) Demonstrate expertise in designated profession. 2) Provide credentials and qualifications. 3) Employment offer or business plan in Thailand (varies). 4) Application more thorough (1-2 months typical). 5) Receive 4-year permission if approved.

Renewal

LTR visa renews every 4 years. Process is simpler than initial application. You provide:

  • Updated bank statement (if wealth-based) showing 3M+ THB still in account
  • Or recent income proof (if income-based)
  • Current passport and completed forms

Processing: 2-3 days. Cost: Free for wealth/income pathways. Elite members pay annual maintenance if applicable.

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • 4-year validity (longest initial grant of any Thai visa)
  • Renewable for additional 4-year periods indefinitely
  • Multiple pathways to suit different financial situations
  • Most prestigious visa status in Thailand
  • No work restrictions on visa itself
  • Can travel freely in and out of Thailand

Cons

  • Higher financial requirements than other visas
  • 3M THB wealth-based funds must stay in Thai bank
  • Elite pathway expensive upfront (600K - 2M THB)
  • Professional/Expert pathway competitive and slow
  • More documentation required at application
Guru Tip: If you have 3M THB to spare and no immediate need for it, wealth-based LTR is straightforward. Deposit, wait 3 months, apply. 4-year peace of mind. If you're a high-income earner (80K+ monthly), income-based is free and simple. Elite membership worth it if you want concierge support and fastest approval. Most people choosing LTR go wealth-based or income-based. Professional pathway is for specialists only.

LTR vs. Other Long-Stay Visas

  • LTR vs. Retirement Visa: LTR requires more capital (3M vs 800K) but 4 years validity vs 1 year. LTR more prestigious.
  • LTR vs. DTV: LTR longer validity (4 years vs 180 days). DTV lower requirements. LTR for wealthier applicants.
  • LTR vs. ED Visa: LTR longest validity. ED visa only 90 days initial. LTR for those who don't want to study.

Key Takeaways

  • LTR: Long Term Resident visa. 4-year initial grant, renewable for 4-year periods indefinitely.
  • Four main pathways: Elite membership, Wealth-based (3M THB), Income-based (80K/month), Professional/Expert.
  • Wealth-based LTR straightforward if you have capital. Deposit 3M THB, apply, get 4-year visa.
  • Income-based LTR free if you earn 80K+ THB monthly. No capital requirement.
  • Most prestigious long-stay visa in Thailand. Elite membership fastest but most expensive.

Guru Tip

The LTR Wealthy Pensioner category is the most underutilised option for retirees with passive income. The 80,000 USD income or 40,000 USD income with 250,000 USD insurance threshold sounds high but includes income from investments, pensions, and superannuation combined. Many Australian retirees who assume they do not qualify actually do when they add all sources. The BOI processes LTR applications faster than standard immigration, typically within 20 business days. Use the BOI online portal to apply rather than going through an agent for your first inquiry.

What is the LTR visa Thailand?

The Long-Term Resident (LTR) visa is a 10-year multiple-entry visa administered by the Board of Investment (BOI). It targets high-net-worth individuals, pensioners with passive income, remote workers for foreign companies, and highly skilled professionals. It offers a 10-year stay with multiple entry rights and certain tax benefits.

What are the LTR visa categories?

Four main categories: Wealthy Global Citizen (1 million USD assets), Wealthy Pensioner (passive income 80,000 USD/year or 40,000 USD with health insurance), Work-from-Thailand Professional (income from overseas company), and Highly Skilled Professional (specialist employment in target industries). Each has specific financial and documentation requirements.

How long does the LTR visa last?

10 years, renewable. It comes with multiple-entry rights, a 10-year work permit (for eligible categories), and no 90-day reporting requirement, which is a significant administrative advantage over standard long-stay visas.

Is there a 90-day reporting requirement for LTR visa holders?

No. This is one of the key practical advantages of the LTR over retirement and ED visas. LTR holders are exempt from the 90-day in-person or online reporting cycle. Combined with the 10-year validity, it removes most of the ongoing administrative burden of long-term residence in Thailand.

Can I work in Thailand on an LTR visa?

The Work-from-Thailand Professional and Highly Skilled Professional categories come with a 10-year work permit. The Wealthy Global Citizen and Wealthy Pensioner categories do not include a standard work permit but allow working for overseas employers remotely. Working for Thai companies or locally earning income requires a separate legal structure.
