Quick Summary (TL;DR)

  • Chiang Mai has verified social events every day of the week. This is not a list of aspirational meetups. Everything here was running as of 2026.
  • Subscribe to Steve's Newsletter (free) for the weekly roundup. It is the single best way to know what is actually happening. Link is in the article.
  • Most events cost nothing or under 300 THB (~$13 AUD). Thai class with Kru Sanee is 250 THB donation. Bachata at Bailamos is 400 THB walk-in.
  • The Expat Breakfast Club on the first Saturday of each month is the fastest way to meet long-term residents. Around 100 people show up. No membership required.
  • Language exchange at The Moat House Rooftop Bar runs every Tuesday from 7pm. Free. One of the best low-pressure ways into the social scene for new arrivals.

Chiang Mai has one of the most active expat social scenes in Southeast Asia. Whatever you are here for, however long you are staying, and whatever you are into, there is a weekly event for you. The list below is updated and verified. If something is wrong or no longer running, let us know.

For the best weekly email roundup of events: subscribe to Steve's Newsletter. General community discussion: Chiang Mai Events Facebook Group.

Social Groups & Networks

Chiang Mai is a great place. You already know that and that's why you are here. But what makes it even more special is the social scene. Whether you're here short or long term, big budget or small, introvert or extrovert, there is a group or network for you. If you already have friends, that's great but don't miss the chance to expand your horizons. There are amazing people everywhere. So get out there and meet some people. And if you don't like a group, there are plenty of others to try.

I've listed as many groups as I can find from Facebook and around the city. Facebook is a great connector, but there are also Meetup networks and more. This includes social groups and other good information.

Every Day: Court Sports & Combat Training

Padel Courts (Multiple Venues, Daily)

Padel runs nearly every day at multiple clubs throughout Chiang Mai. Sessions typically range from 08:00 to sunset. Court hire costs 800-1000 THB/hour. Most clubs welcome players of all levels. For real-time availability and to book courts:
Download ReClub App | Playtomic (Padel Club Chiang Mai)

Pickleball Clubs (Multiple Venues, Daily)

Pickleball matches and open play happen nearly every day at multiple clubs with 8+ courts total across the city. Sessions vary by club and skill level (beginners to advanced). 30-250 THB per hour depending on club and court type. Chiang Mai Pickleball is the most affordable at 30 THB/hour since March 2026. For current schedules and to find players:
Download ReClub App - Search for "Chiang Mai Pickleball," "Imperial Chiang Mai," or "All Time Pickleball"

Combat Training: Muay Thai, Boxing, MMA & Self-Defence (Daily)

Training runs 2-3 sessions daily: early morning (6:30-8:00am) and afternoon (4:00-5:30pm). Single session 300-600 THB. Monthly unlimited 10,000 THB. Programmes include Muay Thai, Western Boxing, MMA, Jeet Kune Do, Filipino Martial Arts, and Emergency Self-Defence. Drop-in welcome. ED visas available for long-term training.
Core Combat Chiang Mai | +66 (0)64 460 9001

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the easiest way to meet people in Chiang Mai as a newcomer?

The CMU Campus Walk every Monday at 5 PM is one of the most open and consistent events in the city. No booking, no cost, just show up. The Expat Breakfast Club runs regular morning events that attract a mix of long-term residents and new arrivals.

Are there social events in Chiang Mai that run every week without booking?

Yes. Monday padel and hash runs (every second Monday), yoga sessions, the CMU walk, Thursday and Saturday hash runs, Saturday markets, and Sunday brunch events all run weekly. No booking required for most events just show up. The full list with times and locations is in this guide.

Is Chiang Mai social life dominated by digital nomads or longer-term expats?

Both communities exist and overlap. Nimman attracts the nomad crowd. The Expat Breakfast Club and longer-running community groups lean toward residents of 6 months or more. The weekly events list covers both.

How do I find out about one-off events and new openings in Chiang Mai?

Steve's Weekly Email is the most reliable source. It goes out each week and covers events, restaurant openings, and community notices that don't appear on Google.

Is it hard to make friends in Chiang Mai if you don't drink?

No. The social scene is unusually varied for its size. Yoga communities, running groups, language exchanges, and sports clubs are all active and none require alcohol. The padel and hash communities in particular are non-drinking-centric.

Guru Tip

The first two weeks in Chiang Mai can feel isolating if you wait for things to happen. Show up to one recurring weekly event and go back the following week. Regularity matters more than effort. After three visits you will have a social circle; after three months you will have more commitments than you can keep.
