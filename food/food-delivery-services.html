Food Delivery Services in Chiang Mai are so EASY!

Food Delivery Service in Chiang Mai makes ordering easy, with options like Grab, Lineman, 7-Eleven, Shopee and Meals on Wheels for every need.

Category Home / Chiang Mai Daily Life
Read time 7 min
Last updated May 2026

Quick Facts

  • Apps: Grab Food, LINE MAN, Foodpanda
  • Delivery fee: 15 - 50 THB typical within city
  • 7-Eleven delivery: Available via LINE MAN
  • Meal plans: Available through local kitchens and services
  • Coverage: Broad across Old City, Nimman, Hang Dong, most urban zones
  • Payment: Cash on delivery or in-app card/QR payment

Food Delivery Services in Chiang Mai: What Actually Works

Chiang Mai is not Bangkok. The delivery infrastructure is smaller, the coverage outside the city core can be patchy, and not every restaurant on the apps is worth ordering from. But if you know which platforms to use and how to use them well, food delivery in Chiang Mai is genuinely practical, whether you are a resident who wants dinner without traffic or a visitor who needs breakfast sent to an Airbnb.

This is not a rehash of what the apps say about themselves. This is how the system actually works for people living here.

The Main Delivery Apps

Three platforms dominate in Chiang Mai. Each has strengths and gaps worth knowing before you commit to one as your default.

Grab Food is the most widely used app in Chiang Mai among expats and long-term visitors. Coverage reaches most of the inner city and a good portion of the outer suburbs. The interface is clear, English-language support is solid, and the driver network is generally reliable. During peak hours, wait times stretch, but tracking is accurate. Grab also integrates with GrabPay, which simplifies cashless orders.

LINE MAN expanded from Bangkok and has grown steadily in Chiang Mai. Its particular advantage is the 7-Eleven integration. You can order convenience store items through LINE MAN and get them delivered in under 30 minutes in most city zones, which is useful when you want something specific rather than a full meal. The restaurant selection on LINE MAN is also strong, with a number of smaller Thai places that do not appear on Grab.

Foodpanda operates in Chiang Mai but with thinner coverage compared to the other two. It is worth checking during promotions, as Foodpanda frequently runs discount codes that make it competitive on price for specific restaurant chains. Outside of those windows, Grab and LINE MAN generally offer better selection and faster delivery times.

Meal Delivery Plans: Regular Kitchen Services

Beyond the apps, Chiang Mai has a growing number of local services offering weekly or monthly meal plans. These tend to be run by small kitchens, often by expats or by Thai cooks with a loyal customer base built through word of mouth.

Chef Fuji is one example that has developed a following in the expat community. The model is simple: a rotating menu of home-style cooking delivered on a schedule. You get consistent quality without the unpredictability of ordering blind on an app. Pricing for these services varies but typically runs 80 to 150 THB per meal, which is reasonable for what you receive.

The best way to find these services is through Chiang Mai expat Facebook groups, where recommendations surface regularly and unhappy customers are equally vocal. If a kitchen service has stayed active for two or more years with consistent positive feedback, it is worth trying.

Shop Delivery: 7-Eleven and Convenience Stores

LINE MAN's 7-Eleven integration deserves its own mention because it is genuinely useful and underused by newcomers. You can order snacks, drinks, ready meals, and basic groceries from your nearest 7-Eleven and have them delivered for a small fee. In Chiang Mai, where 7-Elevens are never far away, this covers a lot of last-minute needs without requiring a trip outside.

This is particularly practical during the rainy season (June to October) when afternoon downpours make short trips inconvenient, or during peak smoky season (February to April) when minimising time outdoors is sensible air quality management.

Coverage and Limitations

If you live or stay within the central ring of Chiang Mai covering the Old City, Nimmanhaemin, Chang Klan, and the Riverside area, all three major apps will serve you reliably. Coverage in Hang Dong, Mae Hia, and the areas near international schools is reasonable but not comprehensive. Further out, towards Mae Rim or San Sai, Grab is your most reliable option, and even there you will occasionally encounter no driver availability during quiet hours.

Some restaurants only appear on one platform. If you have a specific place in mind, check all three apps before assuming it is not available for delivery.

Guru Tip

LINE MAN's 7-Eleven delivery is the least-known practical tool for Chiang Mai residents. Use it for late-night snack runs, morning coffee when you cannot face traffic, or last-minute ingredient gaps when cooking. It arrives faster than ordering from a restaurant and the selection at Chiang Mai's larger 7-Elevens is broader than most people expect. Set up your nearest branch as a favourite location and it becomes a one-tap solve for a lot of minor domestic problems.

Which food delivery app is best in Chiang Mai?

Grab Food has the broadest coverage and the most reliable driver network for most of the city. LINE MAN is strong for 7-Eleven deliveries and offers some restaurants not on Grab. Foodpanda is worth using during promotions but has thinner general coverage.

Can I get 7-Eleven delivered in Chiang Mai?

Yes, through LINE MAN. Select 7-Eleven from the app, choose your nearest branch, and order from the available inventory. Delivery typically arrives within 20 to 30 minutes in central zones.

What does delivery cost in Chiang Mai?

Delivery fees on the main apps range from 15 to 50 THB depending on distance. Some restaurants offer free delivery above a minimum order value. Meal plan services charge per meal rather than per delivery and often include delivery in the price.

Are there meal plan delivery services in Chiang Mai?

Yes. Several small local kitchens offer weekly or monthly meal plans delivered on a schedule. These are often found through Chiang Mai expat Facebook groups. Chef Fuji is one service with a long-standing following. Prices typically range from 80 to 150 THB per meal.

Does food delivery reach outside the city centre?

Coverage drops off past the inner suburbs. Hang Dong and Mae Hia are reasonably served by Grab. Mae Rim and San Sai have limited coverage, particularly outside peak hours. If you live in an outer area, check the app's coverage map for your address specifically.
